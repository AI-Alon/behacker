QR Code Redirect (QRishing)

What is it?

QRishing (QR phishing) is a social engineering attack where malicious QR codes replace or overlay legitimate ones in physical locations. Unlike URLs, QR codes are not human-readable — victims cannot evaluate the destination before scanning. The attacker's domain, not the legitimate service, handles the request.

Why QR codes are dangerous

  • Opaque by design: The encoded URL is invisible without a scanner
  • Physical trust: A QR code on a physical sign appears authoritative
  • No hover preview: Unlike HTML links, there's no way to preview destination
  • Mobile context: Phones scan QR codes directly — security tools are less prevalent

Attack vectors

Physical overlay attack

  1. Print a sticker with a malicious QR code
  2. Place it over the legitimate QR on a parking meter, restaurant menu, or office poster
  3. Victim scans → phishing page → credential capture

Package/delivery attack

"Your package could not be delivered. Scan to reschedule." — fake delivery QR in mailboxes

Conference/event attack

Replace QR codes on name badges, agenda sheets, or WiFi login posters

Payment QR fraud

Replace merchant payment QR with attacker's QR — customer pays attacker

Real-world incidents

  • US FBI warning (2022): Alert about QR code fraud at parking meters
  • Germany (2022): Fake QR codes on charging stations redirected to phishing sites
  • China: Widespread payment QR fraud — victims pay attackers believing they're paying merchants

Defenses for users

  1. Use a QR scanner app that shows the URL before navigating
  2. Verify QR codes on physical payment terminals match the expected merchant
  3. Be suspicious of QR codes that appear to be stickers added on top of printed material
  4. If a QR code leads to a login page, manually navigate to the real site instead

Defenses for organizations

  1. Use QR codes with embedded logos and tamper-evident materials
  2. Regularly inspect physical QR codes for overlays
  3. Use short-lived, signed QR codes for sensitive operations
  4. Monitor for domain registrations that impersonate your brand
