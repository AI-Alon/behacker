Pretexting

What is it?

Pretexting is a social engineering technique where the attacker fabricates a scenario (the "pretext") to manipulate a target into revealing information or taking an action they otherwise wouldn't. Unlike phishing (which relies on urgency and a fake login page), pretexting builds a believable narrative to establish trust or authority.

Core psychological levers

  • Authority: "I'm the CEO / IT Security / your manager"
  • Urgency: "Your account will be locked in 24 hours"
  • Fear: "We detected suspicious activity on your account"
  • Social proof: "All other employees have already completed this"
  • Reciprocity: "We've already verified half your details, just need X"
  • Scarcity: "This is a one-time verification window"

Common pretext scenarios

IT support fraud

"We detected malware on your machine. I need remote access and your current password to remediate."

CEO fraud / Business Email Compromise (BEC)

Impersonate an executive to request urgent wire transfers. BEC caused $2.7 billion in losses in 2022 (FBI IC3 report).

Vendor account change

"We've updated our banking details. Please redirect payment for invoice #X to the new account."

Security audit

"As part of compliance, we need you to verify your credentials by replying to this email."

Red flags

  • Requests for passwords (no legitimate service ever needs your password)
  • Extreme urgency combined with secrecy ("don't tell anyone")
  • Sender email domain slightly different from the real company
  • Requests that bypass normal approval processes

Defenses

  1. Verify through a separate channel: Call the person using a known phone number, not one provided in the suspicious message
  2. Process controls: Wire transfers require multi-person approval + out-of-band confirmation
  3. Training: Regular social engineering awareness training with simulated attacks
  4. "It's okay to say no": Empower employees to refuse unusual requests without fear
  5. Callback verification: For account/banking changes, always call back using a number from official records
